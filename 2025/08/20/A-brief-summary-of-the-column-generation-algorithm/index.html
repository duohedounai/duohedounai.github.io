A Brief Summary of Column Generation

Posted on 2025-08-20 Edited on 2025-08-21 Views: Word count in article: 634 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

Column Generation (CG) is used to solve large-scale linear programming problems with many decision variables. The basic idea behind column generation algorithms is to first consider a subset of the decision variables and then gradually add more decision variables to the model that may lead to better solutions.

Let us take Cutting Stock Problem as an example to explain the solution process of column generation.

The length and corresponding cost of wood raw materials are listed below:

Length of wood raw materials/m Cost
9 5
14 9
16 10

The length and quantity of wood required by the customer are listed below:

Length of wood required by the customer/m Required quantity
4 30
5 20
7 40

Obviously, based on experience we can list some cutting patterns for each length of wood material:

Cutting patterns of 9m wood raw material:

P3 0 0 1 5

Therefore, we can list the mathematical model of this problem. The optimization goal of this problem is to minimize the total cost while meeting customer demand for wood products of different lengths.

As the length types of wood raw materials and wood required by customers increase, the number of decision variables will increase. A simple idea is to write all decision variables in a model and then solve the model directly. For large-scale problems, this is obviously inefficient, which is why we need column generation.

We only consider the problem consisting of a subset of the decision variables of the Master Problem (MP), and this problem is called Restricted Master Problem (RMP).

Next, we can linearly relax the RMP solve it to get the dual cost corresponding to each constraint. Then we can use the dual cost to solve the following sub-problem called the pricing problem to find columns that can enter the basis and reduce the objective function (for a minimization problem).

Branch and price is a hybrid of branch and bound and column generation methods.
