A Common Mathematical Model for Truck Dispatching in Mining Areas

Posted on 2025-04-04 Edited on 2025-08-07 Views: Word count in article: 567 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

I have recently read some literature on truck scheduling in mining areas which is generally a combinatorial optimization problem. The following is a brief summary of a common scheduling model in the literature.

Mathematical Model

Assumptions

(1) All trucks are of the same type and do not break down.
(2) Ignore the waiting time of trucks in queues.

(3) The distance between any loading point and any unloading point is the optimal distance.

(4) All trucks have the same loading and unloading times at loading and unloading points.

Objective Function

Minimize total transportation costs while meeting target mineral production:

Constraints

(1) The production capacity meets the requirements of each unloading area:

∑r=1K∑i=1mC⋅xrij≥fj,∀j\sum_{r=1}^K \sum_{i=1}^m C \cdot x_{rij} \ge f_j, \forall j r=1∑K​i=1∑m​C⋅xrij​≥fj​,∀j

(2) The total amount of minerals transported from loading area iii cannot be greater than its total amount:

∑r=1K∑j=1nC⋅xrij≤gi,∀i\sum_{r=1}^K \sum_{j=1}^n C \cdot x_{rij} \le g_i, \forall i r=1∑K​j=1∑n​C⋅xrij​≤gi​,∀i

(3) The total unloading amount of unloading area jjj cannot be greater than its maximum unloading amount:

∑r=1K∑i=1mC⋅xrij≤hj,∀j\sum_{r=1}^K \sum_{i=1}^m C \cdot x_{rij} \le h_j, \forall j r=1∑K​i=1∑m​C⋅xrij​≤hj​,∀j

(4) The total number of loadings in all loading areas is less than the maximum number of loadings in the current shift:

∑r=1K∑j=1nxrij≤Bc,∀i\sum_{r=1}^K \sum_{j=1}^n x_{rij} \le B_c, \forall i r=1∑K​j=1∑n​xrij​≤Bc​,∀i

(5) The grade of minerals in the unloading areas must meet the given tolerance:
